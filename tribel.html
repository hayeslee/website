Background

During my co-op term at TD Lab, I had the opportunity to work on a personal project catered towards my interests. My boss, David, gave me a topic and from there, I followed the double diamond design process and designed a solution to a problem with respect to the topic. I presented my work at the end of the term in front of the lab. The topic given was music creation collaboration applications.

Research

COMPETITIVE ANALYSIS

To understand the problem and what’s currently out there, I performed a competitive analysis on some of the most popular mobile and web apps within the realm of music creation and collaboration.

The top competitors found were Bandlab, Bandhub, Kompoz, Blend and Trackd. These five applications were similar to one another but each had something unique that they brought to the table. For example, Trackd focused on simplicity, on-the-go mobile music creation whereas Blend was a more advanced application focused on music production.

PRIMARY RESEARCH

After learning a bit more about these apps, I decided to create a survey using Google Forms to understand my potential user base more. Below were questions that I wanted to answer.

  • Do people know about music creation collaboration apps?
  • Which apps have they heard of before?
  • Why do people choose to use/not use music creation collaboration apps?
  • If one were to use a music collaboration app, what would they value?

I surveyed 25 musically-inclined students from various a cappella groups at the University of Waterloo which contributed to some bias in the survey, since most participants were vocalists.

Insights and Opportunities

Through the persona creation, customer journey map and research, some insights were collected:

  • Lots of web apps for collaboration but not many on mobile
  • Collaboration can take different forms but there are 2 common models
    • Record your own part in your own time and contribute to a project
    • Take another project and edit it, giving credit to the original
  • Many use subscription paying models
  • Many people value performance over creation
  • Most apps assume prior experience to using software - there seems to be an emphasis on music production (which makes sense)

Problem Statement

How might we create a convenient and fun experience for musicians of all skill levels to collaborate and/or perform music together regardless of location?
