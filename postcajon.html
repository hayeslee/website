The Post Cajon

Background

As part of our third year design course, we were to take an existing traditional instrument and improve upon it. In terms of what aspect we were to improve upon, it was up to our research and process, with the one requirement being that the instrument must be augmented digitally in some way. We worked in groups of 6 and used the Google Design Sprint to guide our process. My group chose to redesign the cajon. Below are the steps in which we took to complete it.

Choosing our Instrument

To choose an instrument, we decided to do some research to see what would be feasible. We voted on all the choices and chose the instrument with the majority of the group agreeing to it. Here is a list of all the final choices we had:

  • Bagpipes
  • Cajon
  • Rainstick
  • Drum machine extension for beatboxing
  • Lute

We chose the cajon in the end because it was simple enough to build by the end of the term (~3 months) and there was a lot of freedom for us to improve upon it. We researched on some of the

  • Posture Problems
  • Wrist Strain
  • Hand injuries for beginners due to sharp corners
  • Sound limitations
  • Height issues
